\subsection{Motivation from Materials Science}
\subsection*{来自材料科学的动机}

In materials science, \textbf{phase field methods} provide a unified framework
for describing the spatiotemporal evolution of microstructures — the spatial
arrangement of different phases that determines macroscopic material properties
\cite{chen2002, provatas2011}. The key insight is to replace sharp interfaces
with \textbf{diffuse interfaces} described by continuous order parameter fields,
and to let these fields evolve according to thermodynamic driving forces encoded
in a free energy functional.

The two foundational equations of phase field theory, classified as Model A and
Model B in the Hohenberg-Halperin dynamical universality scheme \cite{hohenberg1977}, are:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
    \item \textbf{Allen-Cahn equation} (Model A, non-conserved):
    \begin{equation}
        \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t} = -M_\phi \frac{\delta \cF}{\delta \phi}
        \label{eq:ac_classic}
    \end{equation}
    Describes the evolution of a non-conserved field $\phi$ (e.g., crystal
    orientation, magnetic spin). The dynamics produce domain growth via
    curvature-driven interfacial motion — boundaries move to reduce total
    interface area. The Allen-Cahn equation converges to mean curvature flow
    in the sharp-interface limit \cite{evans1992, ilmanen1993}.

    \item \textbf{Cahn-Hilliard equation} (Model B, conserved):
    \begin{equation}
        \frac{\partial c}{\partial t} = \nabla \cdot \left(M_c(c) \nabla
        \frac{\delta \cF}{\delta c}\right)
        \label{eq:ch_classic}
    \end{equation}
    Describes the evolution of a conserved field $c$ where $\int c\,dx$ is constant.
    The dynamics produce spinodal decomposition and coarsening — the system
    phase-separates into domains that grow via diffusion-limited Ostwald ripening,
    with the celebrated $t^{1/3}$ scaling \cite{lifshitz1961, wagner1961}.
\end{enumerate}

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{在材料科学中，相场方法提供了描述微结构时空演化的统一框架。两个基础方程：Allen-Cahn（非守恒，Model A）——产生曲率驱动的畴壁运动；Cahn-Hilliard（守恒，Model B）——产生旋节分解和$t^{1/3}$粗化。}

\begin{keyeq}

    \cF[\gf, \Sf] = \int_\cM \left[
        f_g(\gf) + \frac{\kappa_g}{2} |\nabla \gf|^2
        + f_S(\Sf) + \frac{\kappa_S}{2} |\nabla \Sf|^2

    \right] dx
    \label{eq:total_free_energy}
\end{equation}
\end{keyeq}

where:
\begin{itemize}

    \item $\frac{\kappa_S}{2}|\nabla\Sf|^2$ — \textbf{potential gradient energy},

    $\Sf<0$ (high potential amplifies bias; low potential does not).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\section{Allen-Cahn Dynamics for the Gauge Field}
\section*{规范场的Allen-Cahn动力学}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Equation of Motion}
\subsection*{运动方程}

The gauge field $\gf$ is a \textbf{non-conserved} order parameter — there is
no conservation law for total ``bias.'' Agents can shift between honest and
dishonest without global constraint. Therefore $\gf$ evolves via
\textbf{Allen-Cahn (Model A)} dynamics:

\begin{definition}[Gauge Allen-Cahn Equation / 规范Allen-Cahn方程]
\label{def:gauge_ac}
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\partial \gf}{\partial t} = -M_g \frac{\delta \cF}{\delta \gf}
    = -M_g \Big[ (A\|\gf\|^2 - B)\gf - \kappa_g \lap \gf + 2\lambda \Sf \, \gf \Big]
    \label{eq:gauge_ac}
\end{equation}
where $M_g>0$ is the gauge mobility.
\end{definition}

The variational derivative decomposes into three forces:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\delta \cF}{\delta \gf}
    = \underbrace{(A\|\gf\|^2 - B)\gf}_{\text{double-well force}}
    - \underbrace{\kappa_g \lap \gf}_{\text{diffusion}}
    + \underbrace{2\lambda \Sf \gf}_{\text{coupling force}}
    \label{eq:variational_g}
\end{equation}

The double-well force drives $\gf$ toward the nearest minimum ($\mathbf{0}$
or $\pm\sqrt{B/A}$). The diffusion term smooths spatial variations,
producing domain walls of finite thickness. The coupling force
$2\lambda\Sf\gf$ is the crucial link: when $\Sf>0$ (above baseline),
it \textbf{amplifies} bias — high potential reinforces dishonesty.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{规范场通过Allen-Cahn动力学演化，三个力分量：双阱力驱动向最近极小值、扩散项平滑空间变化、耦合力$2\lambda\Sf\gf$——高势能放大偏差。}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Interface Structure: The Gauge Domain Wall}
\subsection*{界面结构：规范畴壁}

Consider a 1D stationary interface with $\Sf$ constant and $\lambda\to0$.
The equilibrium profile satisfies:

\begin{equation}
    \kappa_g \frac{d^2 \gf}{dx^2} = \frac{\partial f_g}{\partial \gf}
    \label{eq:equilibrium_wall}
\end{equation}

The classical Allen-Cahn kink solution is:

\begin{theorem}[Gauge Domain Wall Profile / 规范畴壁轮廓]
\label{thm:kink}
The 1D equilibrium profile connecting honest ($\gf=0$ at $x\to-\infty$) to
dishonest ($\|\gf\|=\sqrt{B/A}$ at $x\to+\infty$) is:
\begin{equation}
    \|\gf(x)\| = \sqrt{\frac{B}{A}} \cdot
    \frac{1}{2}\left[1 + \tanh\left(\frac{x - x_0}{\deltac}\right)\right]
    \label{eq:kink_profile}
\end{equation}
with \textbf{domain wall thickness}:
\begin{equation}
    \deltac = \sqrt{\frac{2\kappa_g}{B}}
    \label{eq:wall_thickness}
\end{equation}
and \textbf{interface energy} (energy per unit area):
\begin{equation}
    \sigma_g = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \left[f_g(\gf) + \frac{\kappa_g}{2}\left|\frac{d\gf}{dx}\right|^2\right]dx
    = \frac{2\sqrt{2\kappa_g}}{3}\cdot\frac{B^{3/2}}{A}
    \label{eq:interface_energy}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The first integral of Eq.~\eqref{eq:equilibrium_wall} gives
$\frac{\kappa_g}{2}(d\gf/dx)^2 = f_g(\gf)$.
The kink profile follows by integration. The interface energy uses
$\sigma_g = 2\int_0^{g_{\text{eq}}}\sqrt{2\kappa_g f_g}\,d\gf$.
\end{proof}

\textbf{Social interpretation:} $\deltac$ large = fuzzy moral boundaries
(tolerant society with gradual transitions); $\deltac$ small = sharp
moral boundaries (polarized society). $\sigma_g$ is the social cost of
being at a boundary — the ``cognitive dissonance'' of straddling
honest and dishonest communities.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{经典Allen-Cahn扭结解：$\|\gf(x)\|=\sqrt{B/A}\cdot\frac{1}{2}[1+\tanh((x-x_0)/\deltac)]$。畴壁厚度 $\deltac=\sqrt{2\kappa_g/B}$，界面能 $\sigma_g=\frac{2\sqrt{2\kappa_g}}{3}B^{3/2}/A$。社会意义：$\deltac$大=模糊道德边界，$\sigma_g$=处于界面的社会成本。}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Curvature-Driven Domain Wall Motion}
\subsection*{曲率驱动的畴壁运动}

For curved domain walls in 2D/3D, the Allen-Cahn dynamics reduce to
\textbf{mean curvature flow} in the sharp-interface limit ($\deltac\to0$):

\begin{theorem}[Curvature-Driven Wall Motion / 曲率驱动畴壁运动]
\label{thm:curvature_motion}
Let $\Gamma(t)$ be the interface between honest and dishonest domains.
In the sharp-interface limit, the normal velocity is:
\begin{equation}
    v_n = -M_g \sigma_g \, \kappa
    \label{eq:curvature_flow}
\end{equation}
where $\kappa$ is the mean curvature of $\Gamma(t)$. The interface moves
toward its center of curvature, reducing total interface area.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Social interpretation:} Isolated dishonest clusters shrink and
disappear (convex boundary moves inward — ``corruption bubbles burst'').
Large dishonest domains are metastable (low curvature → slow shrinkage),
explaining why entrenched corruption can persist for generations while
small scandals collapse quickly.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{尖锐界面极限下Allen-Cahn→平均曲率流。孤立不诚实集群缩小消失（\"腐败泡沫破裂\"），大域低曲率→亚稳持久。}

\section{Cahn-Hilliard Dynamics for the Potential Field}
\section*{势能场的Cahn-Hilliard动力学}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Mass-Conserving Evolution}
\subsection*{质量守恒的演化}

The potential field $\Sf$ is \textbf{conserved}: total potential is constant
over redistribution timescales. Therefore $\Sf$ evolves via
\textbf{Cahn-Hilliard (Model B)} dynamics:

\begin{definition}[Cahn-Hilliard Equation for Potential / 势能Cahn-Hilliard方程]
\label{def:potential_ch}
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\partial \Sf}{\partial t} = \nabla \cdot \left(
        M_S(\Sf) \, \nabla \frac{\delta \cF}{\delta \Sf}
    \right)
    \label{eq:potential_ch}
\end{equation}
where $M_S(\Sf) = M_0(1 + \gamma\Sf)$ is the \textbf{potential mobility}.
The $\gamma$ term encodes the Matthew effect: higher potential → higher mobility → faster accumulation.
\end{definition}

The \textbf{chemical potential} (variational derivative) is:
\begin{equation}
    \mu_S \equiv \frac{\delta \cF}{\delta \Sf}
    = \alpha(\Sf - \Sf_0) + \beta \Sf^3 - \kappa_S \lap \Sf + \lambda \|\gf\|^2
    \label{eq:chemical_potential}
\end{equation}

The four terms represent: (1) linear restoring force toward $\Sf_0$,
(2) nonlinear saturation preventing runaway, (3) diffusion smoothing
sharp gradients, (4) coupling — bias creates a chemical potential
contribution that drives $\Sf$ flow.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{Cahn-Hilliard动力学：质量守恒演化，迁移率含马太效应项 $M_S=M_0(1+\gamma\Sf)$。化学势四分量：线性恢复、非线性饱和、扩散平滑、耦合驱动。}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Spinodal Decomposition of Inequality}
\subsection*{不平等的旋节分解}

The Cahn-Hilliard equation with non-convex $f_S$ exhibits \textbf{spinodal
decomposition}: a homogeneous state with $\Sf(x)=\bar{\Sf}$ is unstable
to infinitesimal perturbations when $f_S''(\bar{\Sf}) < 0$.

\begin{theorem}[Spinodal Instability of Equal Distribution / 平等分布的旋节不稳定性]
\label{thm:spinodal}
For the decoupled Cahn-Hilliard system ($\lambda=0$), the growth rate of
a perturbation of wavenumber $k$ is:
\begin{equation}
    \omega(k) = -M_S k^2 \left[ f_S''(\bar{\Sf}) + \kappa_S k^2 \right]
    \label{eq:dispersion}
\end{equation}
Instability ($\omega>0$) occurs when $f_S''(\bar{\Sf})<0$ and
$k^2 < -f_S''(\bar{\Sf})/\kappa_S$. The fastest-growing mode is at
$k_{\max} = \sqrt{-f_S''(\bar{\Sf})/(2\kappa_S)}$, setting the
characteristic domain size at early times.

The spinodal region for the quartic potential $f_S$ is:
\begin{equation}
    |\bar{\Sf}| < \sqrt{\frac{B_S}{3A_S}}
    \quad \text{where} \quad f_S(\Sf) = \frac{A_S}{4}\Sf^4 - \frac{B_S}{2}\Sf^2
    \label{eq:spinodal_region}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

The spinodal represents the critical inequality threshold beyond which
the equal distribution becomes \textbf{intrinsically unstable} — any
infinitesimal fluctuation in wealth/potential will grow, leading to
phase separation into rich and poor domains. This is the thermodynamic
origin of inequality: it is not merely a statistical outcome but a
\textbf{phase instability}.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{Cahn-Hilliard旋节分解：当$f_S''(\bar{\Sf})<0$时均匀态不稳定。色散关系给出最快增长模$k_{\max}$，设定早期畴尺寸。旋节条件定义了不平等的热力学不稳定性阈值。}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Coupling Effects: The Honesty Dividend}
\subsection*{耦合效应：诚实红利}

When $\lambda>0$, the coupling creates a \textbf{bias-driven potential current}:

\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{J}_{\text{coupling}} = -M_S \nabla (\lambda \|\gf\|^2)
    \label{eq:coupling_current}
\end{equation}

Potential flows \textbf{away} from high-bias regions and \textbf{toward}
low-bias regions. This is the ``honesty dividend'': trustworthy systems
attract resources, investment, and talent. Conversely, in the gauge
equation, the coupling term $2\lambda\Sf\gf$ amplifies bias where potential
is high. This creates a \textbf{positive feedback loop}:
\begin{center}

    \node (S) at (0,1.5) {$\Sf\uparrow$};
    \node (g) at (2.5,1.5) {$\|\gf\|\uparrow$};
    \node (J) at (5,1.5) {$\mathbf{J}_{\text{coupling}}$};
    \node (S2) at (5,0) {$\Sf$ flows to honest regions};
    \node (g2) at (2.5,0) {$\|\gf\|$ amplifies at boundaries};
    \draw[->,thick] (S) -- (g);
    \draw[->,thick] (g) -- (J);
    \draw[->,thick] (J) -- (S2);
    \draw[->,thick] (S2) -- (g2);
    \draw[->,thick] (g2) -- (S);
    \node at (2.5,-1) {\small Runaway → EXPLOSIVE phase};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{耦合产生偏差驱动势能流：势能从高偏差区流向低偏差区——\"诚实红利\"。正反馈环导致失控→爆炸相。}

where $\Sbar = \frac{1}{|\cM|}\int_\cM \Sf(x)\,dx$ is the mean potential.

\subsection{Phase I: EQUALITY (平等相)}

\begin{phasebox}[EQUALITY Phase / 平等相]{phaseblue}
\label{phase:equality}
\textbf{Condition:} $\gbar < g_{\text{crit}}^{(1)}$ and $\Delta_S^2 < \Delta_{\text{crit}}^2$.

\textbf{Characteristics:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\|\gf(x)\|\approx0$ everywhere — agents close to honest.
    \item $\Sf(x)$ smooth and nearly uniform — low inequality, high mobility.
    \item No domain walls or extremely diffuse ($\deltac$ large).
    \item \textbf{Linearly stable}: small perturbations decay exponentially.
    \item Free energy at or near global minimum.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Social interpretation:} A high-trust, well-functioning society with
low corruption and relatively equal distribution of resources. The system
is in the single-phase region — no phase separation. Equilibrium is
the ``rule of law'' attractor.
\end{phasebox}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Phase II: STRATIFIED (分层相)}

\begin{phasebox}[STRATIFIED Phase / 分层相]{phaseyellow}
\label{phase:stratified}
\textbf{Condition:} $\gbar \ge g_{\text{crit}}^{(1)}$ or $\Delta_S^2 \ge \Delta_{\text{crit}}^2$,
but $\gbar < g_{\text{crit}}^{(2)}$.

\textbf{Characteristics:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Gauge phase separation:} Honest and dishonest domains coexist,
          separated by domain walls of thickness $\deltac$.
    \item \textbf{Potential stratification:} $\Sf(x)$ develops plateaus —
          wealthy enclaves and impoverished zones.
    \item \textbf{Metastability:} The system can persist for long times
          but is not true equilibrium — domain walls store interfacial energy.
    \item \textbf{Coarsening:} Larger domains grow at expense of smaller ones
          (Ostwald ripening, Section~7).
    \item Free energy above global minimum but trapped by kinetic barriers.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Social interpretation:} A stratified society with entrenched inequality.
The system is \textbf{metastable} — it can survive for generations, but the
stored interfacial energy makes it vulnerable. This is the ``normal'' state
of most historical societies. The domain walls are the tensions between
classes, regions, and social groups.
\end{phasebox}

\begin{phasebox}[EXPLOSIVE Phase / 爆炸相]{phasered}
\label{phase:explosive}
\textbf{Condition:} $\gbar \ge g_{\text{crit}}^{(2)}$ and $\|\nabla\Sf\|_{\max} \ge G_{\text{crit}}$.

\textbf{Characteristics:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Runaway coupling:} $\lambda$ term dominates. High $\Sf$
          amplifies $\|\gf\|$, which drives $\Sf$ redistribution, which
          further concentrates $\Sf$ and amplifies $\|\gf\|$ at boundaries.
    \item \textbf{Gradient blowup:} $\|\nabla\Sf\|\to\infty$ at interfaces.
          Sharp-interface approximation breaks down.
    \item \textbf{Domain wall collapse:} $\deltac\to0$ — walls become
          infinitely sharp, interface energy diverges.
    \item \textbf{System detonation:} Stored free energy released
          catastrophically — revolution, institutional collapse, audit failure.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Social interpretation:} Revolutionary situation — inequality has
reached critical threshold, bias is rampant, the system cannot contain
interfacial tension. The STRATIFIED→EXPLOSIVE transition is the
\textbf{tipping point} beyond which peaceful resolution is impossible.
This is Thm11's ``attack inevitability'' at the macroscopic scale.
\end{phasebox}

\section{Nucleation and Growth of Audit Domains}
\section*{审计畴的成核与生长}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The Nucleation Problem}
\subsection*{成核问题}

How does an honest domain emerge in a dishonest ``sea''? This is the
\textbf{nucleation problem} of classical phase transition theory. Consider
a system quenched from EQUALITY into STRATIFIED — e.g., a society undergoing
rapid institutional decay. The homogeneous dishonest state is now metastable:
phase separation would lower the free energy, but a small honest cluster
cannot grow unless it exceeds a \textbf{critical size}.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{诚实畴如何在\"不诚实的海洋\"中出现？这是经典相变理论的成核问题。淬火进入分层相后，均匀不诚实态是亚稳的——小诚实簇无法生长除非超过临界尺寸。}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Classical Nucleation Theory}
\subsection*{经典成核理论}

Consider a spherical (in 3D) or circular (in 2D) honest nucleus of radius
$R$, with $\|\gf\|=0$ inside and $\|\gf\|=g_{\text{eq}}=\sqrt{B/A}$ outside.

\begin{theorem}[Nucleation Free Energy / 成核自由能]
\label{thm:nucleation_energy}
The excess free energy relative to the uniform dishonest state is:
\begin{equation}
    \Delta \cF(R) = -\Delta f \cdot V_d(R) + \sigma_g \cdot A_d(R)
    \label{eq:nucleation_energy}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\Delta f = f_g(g_{\text{eq}}) - f_g(0) = B^2/(4A)$ —
          \textbf{bulk free energy gain} per unit volume for converting
          dishonest to honest material.
    \item $\sigma_g$ — \textbf{interface energy} per unit area.
    \item $V_d(R) = \frac{\pi^{d/2}}{\Gamma(d/2+1)} R^d$ — volume.
    \item $A_d(R) = \frac{2\pi^{d/2}}{\Gamma(d/2)} R^{d-1}$ — surface area.
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

Specifically, in the relevant dimensions:
\begin{align}
    \Delta \cF_2(R) &= -\pi R^2 \Delta f + 2\pi R \sigma_g
    \quad\text{(2D: social ``patches'' / 社会\"斑块\")}
    \label{eq:nucleation_2d} \\[4pt]
    \Delta \cF_3(R) &= -\frac{4\pi}{3} R^3 \Delta f + 4\pi R^2 \sigma_g
    \quad\text{(3D: institutional ``spheres'' / 制度\"球体\")}
    \label{eq:nucleation_3d}
\end{align}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Critical Nucleus Size}
\subsection*{临界核尺寸}

\begin{theorem}[Critical Nucleus Radius / 临界核半径]
\label{thm:critical_nucleus}
$\Delta\cF(R)$ has a maximum at the critical radius $\Rc$:
\begin{equation}
    \Rc^{(d)} = \frac{(d-1)\sigma_g}{\Delta f}
    \label{eq:Rc_general}
\end{equation}

In 2D and 3D:
\begin{align}
    \Rc^{(2)} &= \frac{\sigma_g}{\Delta f}
    = \frac{8\sqrt{2\kappa_g}}{3}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{B}}{A^{1/2}}
    \label{eq:Rc_2d} \\[4pt]
    \Rc^{(3)} &= \frac{2\sigma_g}{\Delta f}
    = \frac{16\sqrt{2\kappa_g}}{3}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{B}}{A^{1/2}}
    \label{eq:Rc_3d}
\end{align}

The \textbf{nucleation barrier} (activation energy) is:
\begin{equation}
    \Delta \cF^* \equiv \Delta \cF(\Rc) =
    \begin{cases}
        \pi \sigma_g \Rc = \pi\sigma_g^2/\Delta f & d=2 \\[4pt]
        \dfrac{16\pi}{3}\dfrac{\sigma_g^3}{(\Delta f)^2} & d=3
    \end{cases}
    \label{eq:nucleation_barrier}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Set $\partial_R\Delta\cF_d = -d\cdot c_d R^{d-1}\Delta f + (d-1)\cdot s_d R^{d-2}\sigma_g = 0$
and solve for $R$. The second derivative is negative, confirming a maximum.
\end{proof}

\textbf{This is the central result:} $\Rc = (d-1)\sigma_g/\Delta f$ is the
\textbf{minimum size} an honest cluster must achieve to become self-sustaining.
Below $\Rc$, the social pressure (interface energy cost of being different)
overwhelms the benefit of honesty (bulk free energy gain), and the cluster
is reabsorbed into the dishonest majority. Above $\Rc$, the honest domain
grows spontaneously — a ``reform avalanche.''

The critical radius depends on the ratio $\sigma_g/\Delta f$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item High $\sigma_g$ (strong conformity pressure, thick domain walls $\deltac$ large)
          → large $\Rc$ → \textbf{reform is hard}.
    \item High $\Delta f$ (large payoff for honesty, deep free energy well)
          → small $\Rc$ → \textbf{reform is easy}.
\end{itemize}

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{核心结果：$\Rc = (d-1)\sigma_g/\Delta f$ 是诚实集群变为自持的最小尺寸。$R<\Rc$→缩小消失；$R>\Rc$→自发生长（\"改革雪崩\"）。$\Rc$取决于社会从众压力$\sigma_g$与诚实收益$\Delta f$的比率。}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Nucleation Rate and Connection to Thm10}
\subsection*{成核率与Thm10联系}

The nucleation rate follows the Arrhenius form:
\begin{equation}
    J = J_0 \exp\left(-\frac{\Delta \cF^*}{k_B T_{\text{social}}}\right)
    \label{eq:nucleation_rate}
\end{equation}
where $k_B T_{\text{social}}$ is an effective ``social temperature'' measuring
the intensity of random fluctuations (scandals, information leaks, leadership
changes, external shocks).

\textbf{Connection to Theorem 10 (Boundary Locking):} Thm10 describes a
boundary-confined group where pressure accumulates as $P_\Gamma = \rho_\Gamma \Delta_\Gamma$.
In nucleation terms, this is a \textbf{heterogeneous nucleation} problem:
the boundary $\Gamma$ provides a pre-existing interface that reduces the
effective nucleation barrier. The detonation condition $T_{\text{crit}} \propto 1/\Delta_\Gamma$
is the time for the chemical potential difference to overcome the effective
barrier at the boundary, triggering catastrophic depinning.

\textbf{Crises create reform opportunities:} A scandal (high $T_{\text{social}}$)
lowers the effective barrier, allowing smaller honest clusters to surpass $\Rc$
and grow. This explains why institutional reform often follows crisis — the
``social temperature'' spike reduces the nucleation barrier.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{成核率$J \propto \exp(-\Delta\cF^*/k_BT_{\text{social}})$。高\"社会温度\"降低势垒。Thm10对应异质成核——边界$\Gamma$提供预存界面降低势垒。危机创造改革机会。}

% ===========================================================================
\section{Coarsening Dynamics: Ostwald Ripening}
\section*{粗化动力学：Ostwald熟化}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The Late-Stage Coarsening Regime}
\subsection*{晚期粗化区}

After nucleation produces honest domains and initial growth consumes the
surrounding dishonest ``matrix,'' the system enters the \textbf{late-stage
coarsening regime}. The volume fraction of each phase is near equilibrium,
and further evolution occurs via \textbf{interface reduction} — minimizing
total domain wall area.

The dominant mechanism is \textbf{Ostwald ripening} (Lifshitz-Slyozov-Wagner
coarsening) \cite{lifshitz1961, wagner1961}: larger domains grow at the
expense of smaller ones because the chemical potential at a curved interface
depends on curvature via the Gibbs-Thomson effect.

\bigskip
\noindent

\subsection{LSW $t^{1/3}$ Scaling Law}

\begin{theorem}[Coarsening of Honest Domains / 诚实畴的粗化]
\label{thm:coarsening}
In the late-stage coarsening regime of the coupled Allen-Cahn/Cahn-Hilliard
system with finite volume fraction of the honest phase, the characteristic
domain size $L(t)$ grows as:
\begin{equation}
    L(t) \sim t^{1/3}
    \label{eq:lsw_scaling}
\end{equation}
This is the classical LSW $t^{1/3}$ scaling for diffusion-limited coarsening.
The domain size distribution approaches a universal scaling form
$P(R/\langle R\rangle)$ independent of initial conditions.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Derivation sketch]
Following LSW theory: the growth rate of a domain of radius $R$ in the
late-stage regime is:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{dR}{dt} = \frac{D}{R}\left(\frac{1}{R_c} - \frac{1}{R}\right)
    \label{eq:lsw_growth}
\end{equation}
where $D$ is the effective diffusivity of ``honesty'' through the dishonest
matrix, and $R_c$ is the critical radius (domains with $R=R_c$ neither
grow nor shrink). Mass conservation gives $R_c = \langle R\rangle$.
The asymptotic analysis yields $R_c(t) \propto t^{1/3}$ and a universal
scaled size distribution. For finite volume fractions (typical for social
systems), the scaling remains $t^{1/3}$ but the distribution broadens
\cite{ardell1972}.

Dimensional analysis: flux between domains $J \sim D\cdot\Delta\mu \sim D\cdot\sigma_g/(RL)$.
Growth rate $dR/dt \sim J/R^2 \sim D\sigma_g/(R^3L)$. With $L\sim R$,
$dL/dt \sim D\sigma_g/L^2$, which integrates to $L(t) \sim (D\sigma_g t)^{1/3}$.
\end{proof}

\textbf{Social interpretation — the cubic law of reform:} After a social
quench (revolution, major reform, institutional reset), honest domains
coarsen with $t^{1/3}$ scaling. Achieving large-scale institutional
integrity requires time \textbf{cubic} in the desired domain size:
doubling the characteristic scale of honest governance requires an
$8\times$ increase in duration. This is a sobering prediction:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Building a local honest institution (small $L$) takes years.
    \item Building a national honest institution (medium $L$) takes decades.
    \item Building a global honest institution (large $L$) takes centuries.
\end{itemize}
The $t^{1/3}$ law explains why institutional reform is a generational
project — not because people resist change, but because the \textbf{physics
of coarsening} imposes a cubic timescale.

\bigskip
\noindent
\cn{核心结果：$L(t)\sim t^{1/3}$——改革的立方律。诚实畴尺度翻倍需8倍时间。解释了制度改革为何是跨代工程——不是人心抗拒变革，而是\\textbf{粗化物理学}施加了立方时间尺度。}

The coupled Allen-Cahn/Cahn-Hilliard system is solved on a uniform 2D grid

    -M_g\big[(A(\gf^n)^2 - B)\gf^n + 2\lambda\Sf^n\gf^n\big]

        + \beta(\Sf^n)^3 + \lambda\|\gf^n\|^2
    \big]\big)
\end{align}

In Fourier space, $\widehat{\lap u} = -k^2\hat{u}$, making the implicit
solve trivial: $\hat{u}^{n+1} = (\hat{u}^n + \Delta t\cdot\widehat{\text{NL}})

stable for the linear part and is standard for phase field simulations

\section{Derivation of the LSW $t^{1/3}$ Law}
\section*{LSW $t^{1/3}$ 律的推导}

Following classical LSW theory \cite{lifshitz1961, wagner1961}, in the
late-stage coarsening regime with small volume fraction of the minority
phase, the growth rate of a domain of radius $R$ is:

\begin{equation}
    \frac{dR}{dt} = \frac{D}{R}\left(\frac{1}{R_c} - \frac{1}{R}\right)
    \label{eq:lsw_growth_app}
\end{equation}

where $D$ is effective diffusivity and $R_c$ is the critical radius
($dR/dt|_{R=R_c}=0$). Mass conservation implies $R_c = \langle R\rangle$.
The asymptotic analysis \cite{lifshitz1961} yields $R_c(t) \propto t^{1/3}$
and a universal scaled size distribution $P(\rho)$ with $\rho = R/R_c$:

\begin{equation}
    P(\rho) = \begin{cases}
        \dfrac{81\rho^2 e^{-\rho/(1.5-\rho)}}{2^{5/3}(\rho+3)^{7/3}(1.5-\rho)^{11/3}} & \rho < 1.5 \\
        0 & \rho \ge 1.5
    \end{cases}
\end{equation}

For systems with finite volume fraction (typical for social systems), the
scaling remains $t^{1/3}$ but the distribution broadens \cite{ardell1972}.
