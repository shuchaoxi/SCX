\section{SCX Self-Evolution Theory --- Mathematical Formalization}\label{scx-self-evolution-theory-mathematical-formalization}

\begin{quote}
Formalize the SCX self-evolution closed loop (judge $\to$ store $\to$ update $\to$ re-judge $\to$ re-update $\to$ \ldots) as a rigorous mathematical theory, and establish connections with known theoretical frameworks.
\end{quote}

\textbf{Last updated}: 2026-06-28

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{Problem}\label{problem}

The SCX system forms the following closed loop during operation:

\begin{verbatim}
Judge (S_t scoring) → Store (M_t memory bank) → Update (S_{t+1} re-scoring) → Re-judge → ...
                                ↘ Update (θ_{t+1} NEP student update) ↗
\end{verbatim}

\textbf{Core question}: Does this closed loop converge? To what? At what rate? Under what conditions does it diverge?

\textbf{Difficulty}: The Gatekeeper $S_t$ and the NEP student $f_{\theta_t}$ form a coupled dynamical system --- $S_t$'s selection determines the training distribution of $f_{\theta_t}$, while $f_{\theta_t}$'s output provides feedback to $S_t$.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{Core Insight}\label{core-insight}

\begin{verbatim}
The correct mathematical object for SCX self-evolution is the coupled dynamical system (S_t, θ_t, M_t),
whose global behavior is governed by the monotonic descent property of a Lyapunov function V(S, θ).
\end{verbatim}

Here $S_t: \mathcal{X} \to [0,1]$ is the gatekeeper scoring function, $\theta_t$ is the NEP student parameters, and $M_t$ is the monotonically growing empirical memory bank.

\subsection{Core Theorems}\label{core-theorems}

\subsubsection{Theorem SE-1: Convergence of SCX Self-Evolution}\label{theorem-se-1-convergence-of-scx-self-evolution}

\textbf{Conditions (C1-C7)}:
1. The structure space $\mathbb{X}$ is finite (finite data, finite precision).
2. The NEP student $f_\theta$ is $L_f$-Lipschitz in $\theta$.
3. The Gatekeeper $S_t$ is $L_S$-Lipschitz in its parameters.
4. Learning rates satisfy the Robbins-Monro condition: $\sum \alpha_t = \infty, \sum \alpha_t^2 < \infty$.
5. Given $S_t$, memory bank sampling is i.i.d.
6. Sufficient annealing: $\alpha_t \to 0$, and gatekeeper update frequency $\to 0$.
7. Gatekeeper update step is bounded: $\|S_{t+1} - S_t\|_\infty \leq \eta_t$, $\sum \eta_t < \infty$.

\textbf{Conclusion}: The sequence $(S_t, \theta_t)$ converges almost surely to a joint fixed point $(S^*, \theta^*)$, where:
- $S^*$ is self-consistent: $S^*(x) = \mathbb{P}(\text{correct} \mid x, \mathcal{M}_\infty)$.
- $\theta^*$ is a local minimum of the NEP expected loss under the distribution induced by $S^*$.

\textbf{Proof path}: Lemma SE-1.1 (Lyapunov supermartingale) + Lemma SE-1.2 (finite types) + Lemma SE-1.3 (vanishing step size) + Lemma SE-1.4 (limit points are fixed points).

\subsubsection{Theorem SE-2: Completeness Bound}\label{theorem-se-2-completeness-bound}

Under physical constraints (finite data $N_{\max}$, finite precision $\varepsilon_{\text{mach}}$, finite computation), there exists a finite time $T^*$ such that for all $t \geq T^*$, the system is at an $\varepsilon$-approximate fixed point.

\textbf{I. Classical Convergence} & $(S_t, \theta_t) \to (S^*, \theta^*)$, monotonic improvement & C1-C7 all satisfied, sufficient annealing \\
\textbf{II. Limit Cycle} & System oscillates periodically among finitely many configurations & Insufficient annealing, excessively strong coupling \\
\textbf{III. Perpetual Discovery} & New structures continuously discovered, $\mathcal{M}_t$ grows unboundedly & Open physical world, unlimited exploration budget \\
