\noindent\textbf{English Abstract.}
Climate change is the most complete and merciless instantiation of SCX potential surface geometry at the scale of civilization. The current generation extracts enormous potential energy through greenhouse gas emissions — industrialization, consumption upgrades, lifestyle convenience — and this potential is credited to the current generation's potential surface $\GenS_{\text{now}}$. But the consequences — sea-level rise, extreme weather, ecosystem collapse — are not borne by the current generation. They fall onto the potential surface of future generations $\GenS_{\text{future}}$. This is an \textbf{Intergenerational Potential Jump}: $\Delta_{\text{intergen}} = \GenS_{\text{now}} - \GenS_{\text{future}} > 0$, and $\Delta_{\text{intergen}}$ accumulates over time. Future generations cannot audit, cannot protest, cannot migrate — they are not yet born. This is precisely Theorem 10 (Confinement) at civilization scale with the longest possible lock duration: the confined group (future generations) accumulates pressure they cannot release, the critical time $T_{\text{crit}} \propto 1/\Delta_{\text{intergen}}$, and the burst flow $J_{\text{burst}} \propto \Delta_{\text{intergen}}^2$.
We establish a formal framework for environmental intergenerational potential. We prove: \textbf{(1)} Carbon emissions are potential extraction operations from the Earth system — each ton of carbon corresponds to a potential quantum deducted from the future generations' account; \textbf{(2)} Climate debt is the accumulated $\Delta$ — each year's emission increment manufactures a larger jump on the intergenerational interface; \textbf{(3)} The carbon budget is an interface-smoothing operation — the $2^\circ\text{C}$ target is the critical threshold $\Delta_{\text{crit}}$ for potential jumps, beyond which detonation transitions from metastable to inevitable; \textbf{(4)} Future generations cannot audit their own interests — they do not exist — therefore a \textbf{Trustee-Auditor} is required: states or international institutions must audit from the coordinate system of future generations, or the potential debt will be settled in the form of environmental collapse ($J_{\text{burst}}$); \textbf{(5)} The discount rate $r$ is the $\GParam$ parameter — it determines the weight of future generations' potential in the current coordinate system. The choice of discount rate is not a technical problem — it is a gauge-fixing problem: whose coordinate system defines ``future value''?
This paper is bilingual (Chinese/English) with a complete theorem system, formal proofs, climate debt dynamics equations, ``landmine'' emission analysis, trustee-audit operational protocols, gauge-fixing discussion of the discount rate, and honest critique markings.
