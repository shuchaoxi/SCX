\subsection{Base Manifold: The Claim Space $\ClaimSpace$}

\begin{definition}[Claim Space as a Differentiable Manifold]

\begin{equation}

\end{equation}

The transition functions between claims are:
\begin{equation}
    \phi_\beta \circ \phi_\alpha^{-1}: \phi_\alpha(U_\alpha \cap U_\beta) \to \phi_\beta(U_\alpha \cap U_\beta)

The smoothness of these transition functions defines the differentiable structure of the claim space — i.e., the ability to continuously transition between different claims.
\end{definition}

\begin{itemize}
