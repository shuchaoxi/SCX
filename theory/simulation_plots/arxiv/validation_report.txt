Claim 5 (Lyapunov Phi_t descent) failure analysis:
  The Lyapunov function Phi_t = MSE(S_t, C_ref) does not decrease because
  S_t moves toward the IMPROVED consensus target, not the fixed reference.
  This confirms DEFECT-03/04 from the verification report:
  - The student improves (F1 rises), which changes the optimal consensus target
  - S_t correctly follows the improved target, increasing MSE to the OLD reference
  - This is NOT a bug in the theory — it shows the Lyapunov candidate needs
    a UPDATING reference (importance-weighted) as per Theorem 12.5
  - Or equivalently: the reference-set replay mechanism (C10) is needed
  Theory impact: The Lyapunov descent property IS conditional (conjecture).

Claim 2 (eta_eff decay) failure analysis:
  eta_eff may not decay significantly because:
  - Memory grows monotonically: once a sample enters M_t, it stays forever
  - Early clean samples remain, but early noise also persists
  - With declining threshold, new samples are added but the old ones stay
  - Stronger decay would require a removal mechanism (not in current theory)
  - The fraction of new samples is small relative to total memory size

Claim 7 (Convergence rate) failure analysis:
  Empirical slope (-0.0568) differs from theory (-0.6):
  - The theory rate O(t^{-a}) is asymptotic (t -> inf)
  - At T=20, we are in the transient regime, not asymptotic
  - The exponential bound from Theorem 1 may dominate at small t
  - More iterations (> 100) needed to verify asymptotic rate
  - The beta_t = t^{-0.8} schedule does NOT satisfy Sum(beta_t) < inf
    (theory requires b > 1 for this condition)
