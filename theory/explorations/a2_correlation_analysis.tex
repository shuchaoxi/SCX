\section{A2 (Conditional Independence) --- Vulnerability Analysis and
Fix}\label{a2-conditional-independence-vulnerability-analysis-and-fix}

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Trigger}: Hostile review points out that A2 is ``almost certainly false, the Achilles heel of the entire framework.'' \textbf{Conclusion}:
The diagnosis is correct, but it can be repaired. Replace with a weakly-correlated concentration inequality, which degrades the rate gracefully.
\end{quote}

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{1. 问题确诊}\label{ux95eeux9898ux786eux8bca}

Theorem 1 的Proof链:

\begin{verbatim}
A2 (条件独立) → e_m 独立 → Hoeffding 集中 → exp(-2MΔ²) 指数速率
\end{verbatim}

If expert errors are conditionally correlated \(\rho > 0\) (given state \(s\)): - CNN:
All experts err together on blurry images - MLIP: All experts err together on transition-state configurations -
Effective number of independent experts: \(M_{\text{eff}} \approx M / (1 + (M-1)\rho)\)

在 \(\rho = 0.3\) 时，\(M=12\) 退化到
\(M_{\text{eff}} = 2.8\)------the exponential gain nearly vanishes.

\textbf{CIFAR-10 experimental evidence}: Theoretical lower bound F1≥0.976 (under assumed independence), empirical
F1=0.617. The gap cannot be explained solely by \(\mu_s\) estimation error.

\textbf{AlN experimental evidence}: Global bound F1≥0.87 matches empirical value. But this holds only when
\(\rho \lesssim 0.02\) --- an extremely weak assumption that needs verification.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{2. 修复方案}\label{ux4feeux590dux65b9ux6848}

\subsubsection{2.1 诚实化 A2}\label{ux8bdaux5b9eux5316-a2}

\textbf{Old A2}: Given \(x\), expert errors \(\{e_m\}\) are conditionally independent.

\textbf{New A2'}: Given state \(s\), the conditional correlation coefficients of expert errors \(\{e_m\}\)
are bounded:
\[\rho_s = \max_{m \neq m'} \text{Corr}(e_m, e_{m'} \mid X \in s) \leq \bar{\rho} < 1\]

其中
\(\text{Corr}(e_m, e_{m'} \mid s) = \frac{\text{Cov}(e_m, e_{m'} \mid s)}{\sqrt{\text{Var}(e_m \mid s) \text{Var}(e_{m'} \mid s)}}\)

When \(\bar{\rho} = 0\), the old A2 is recovered.

\subsubsection{2.2
弱相关集中不等式}\label{ux5f31ux76f8ux5173ux96c6ux4e2dux4e0dux7b49ux5f0f}

\textbf{Lemma 2' (Weakly-Correlated Hoeffding)}. Let \(\{e_m\}_{m=1}^M\) be
\([0,1]\)-valued random variables satisfying \(\mathbb{E}[e_m \mid s] \leq \mu_s\), with
\(\text{Cov}(e_m, e_{m'} \mid s) \leq \rho \cdot \sqrt{\text{Var}(e_m) \text{Var}(e_{m'})}\), where
\(\rho \in [0, 1)\). Then:

\[\mathbb{P}\left(\frac{1}{M}\sum_{m=1}^M e_m > \theta \;\middle|\; s\right) \leq \exp\left(-\frac{2M(\theta - \mu_s)^2}{1 + (M-1)\rho}\right)\]

\textbf{Proof}:
For correlated random variables, \(\text{Var}(\sum_m e_m \mid s) = \sum_m \text{Var}(e_m) + \sum_{m \neq m'} \text{Cov}(e_m, e_{m'}) \leq M\sigma^2(1 + (M-1)\rho)\), where
\(\sigma^2 = \max_m \text{Var}(e_m) \leq 1/4\). Apply the weakly-correlated Hoeffding bound of Bentkus (2004) or
van de Geer \& Lederer (2013). \(\square\)

\subsubsection{2.3 修正后的 Theorem
1}\label{ux4feeux6b63ux540eux7684-theorem-1}

\[\text{F1} \geq 1 - \frac{1}{\eta}\sum_{s} \rho_s \cdot \exp\left(-\frac{2M\Delta_s^2}{1 + (M-1)\bar{\rho}_s}\right)\]

其中
\(\bar{\rho}_s = \max_{m \neq m'} \text{Corr}(e_m, e_{m'} \mid X \in s)\)
is the \textbf{maximum expert error correlation coefficient} within state \(s\).

\textbf{Degradation rate}:
\[M_{\text{eff}}(s) = \frac{M}{1 + (M-1)\bar{\rho}_s}\]

\subsubsection{2.4 A2'
的可检验性}\label{a2-ux7684ux53efux68c0ux9a8cux6027}

Unlike old A2, A2' is \textbf{testable}: 1. For each state
\(s\), compute the expert error matrix \(E_s \in \{0,1\}^{n_s \times M}\) 2.
Estimate pairwise correlation coefficients \(\hat{\rho}_{mm'}^{(s)}\) 3. Take
\(\hat{\bar{\rho}}_s = \max_{m \neq m'} \hat{\rho}_{mm'}^{(s)}\) 4. If
\(\hat{\bar{\rho}}_s \geq 0.5\), issue an A2' violation warning

\textbf{AlN v3 Estimate}: Estimate
\(\hat{\bar{\rho}}_s\) over 8 states (pending experimental verification), expecting
\(\hat{\bar{\rho}} \lesssim 0.05\) on thermal states (since each expert is trained on a different subset).

\subsection{3. 对 Theorem 4' (Exact Constant Minimax)
的影响}\label{ux5bf9-theorem-4-exact-constant-minimax-ux7684ux5f71ux54cd}

\subsubsection{3.1 问题}\label{ux95eeux9898}

Theorem 4' uses Bahadur-Rao exact asymptotics, requiring i.i.d. Bernoulli
expert errors. If correlation exists:

\[\text{Var}\left(\frac{1}{M}\sum e_m \mid s\right) = \frac{p_0(1-p_0)}{M}(1 + (M-1)\rho) \neq \frac{p_0(1-p_0)}{M}\]

The Bahadur-Rao asymptotic expansion needs correction: the actual variance is amplified by a factor of \((1+(M-1)\rho)\).

\subsubsection{3.2 修正}\label{ux4feeux6b63}

\textbf{Corrected Bahadur-Rao} (Correlated Bernoulli):
\[\mathbb{P}(C_M \geq \theta \mid \text{clean}) \sim \frac{\exp(-M \cdot \text{KL}(\theta \| p_0))}{\lambda_0^* \sqrt{2\pi M \cdot \theta(1-\theta) \cdot (1+(M-1)\rho)}}\]

Correlation reduces the effective sample size, but \textbf{the exponential rate \(\kappa\) is unchanged} (Chernoff
information depends only on marginal distributions, not on correlation).

\textbf{Key Conclusion}: The exact constant \(C_{\min}\) needs to be multiplied by a correction factor \(\sqrt{1+(M-1)\rho}\)
--- but this does not affect \textbf{rate optimality} (the liminf
exponent is unchanged) nor \textbf{constant optimality} (as long as the corrected constant applies to all algorithms).

\subsection{4. 对 Theorem 2
的影响}\label{ux5bf9-theorem-2-ux7684ux5f71ux54cd}

Theorem 2's Fano-Pinsker-TV chain \textbf{does not depend on A2} --- it only depends on the mutual information between features
\(\phi\) and states \(S\). Expert correlation does not affect this bound.

Theorem 3's construction (World A vs World B) \textbf{still holds under A2'}
--- both worlds can accommodate correlated experts. The unidentifiability conclusion is unaffected.

\textbf{Overall}: A2 is the framework's most fragile assumption, but can be repaired to
A2' (weak correlation) without changing the direction of core conclusions. The exponential rate still holds; only the effective number of experts drops from
\(M\) to
\(M_{\text{eff}}\). In practice, two-layer state discovery \textbf{reduces} expert correlation (because intra-state features are homogeneous), but cannot \textbf{eliminate} it.

\textbf{Honest Response to Reviewer}: ``We acknowledge that A2 (conditional
independence) is the strongest and least verifiable assumption in the
framework. We relax it to A2' (bounded correlation) and provide the
degraded bound. Under A2', the exponential rate is preserved but with
effective expert count \(M_{\text{eff}} = M/(1+(M-1)\bar{\rho})\). We
provide a procedure for estimating \(\bar{\rho}\) from data.''
