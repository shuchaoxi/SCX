\section{A2
--- Rigorous Mathematical Analysis}\label{a2-ux7684ux4e25ux683cux6570ux5b66ux5206ux6790}

\begin{quote}
Trigger: hostile review points out that \(\exp(-2M\Delta^2/(1+(M-1)\rho))\)
is not a rigorous theorem. Conclusion: correct. Plugging variance inflation into Hoeffding
is a heuristic, not a rigorous bound. But A2 itself has stronger justification.
\end{quote}

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{1. \(\exp(-2M t^2/(1+(M-1)\rho))\)
--- Is It a Rigorous Theorem?}{1. \textbackslash exp(-2M t\^{}2/(1+(M-1)\textbackslash rho)) --- Is It a Rigorous Theorem?}}\label{exp-2m-t21m-1rho-ux662fux4e0dux662fux4e25ux683cux5b9aux7406}

\textbf{No.}

The proof of Hoeffding's inequality depends on:
\[\mathbb{E}\left[\exp\left(\lambda\sum_{i=1}^M (X_i - \mu_i)\right)\right] = \prod_{i=1}^M \mathbb{E}[\exp(\lambda(X_i - \mu_i))]\]

This \textbf{factorization} requires \(\{X_i\}\) to be independent. If \(X_i\) are correlated, the MGF
cannot be factorized, and you cannot simply substitute \(M/(1+(M-1)\rho)\) for \(M\)
to obtain a rigorous exponential bound.

Variance inflation \((1+(M-1)\rho)\) is correct for \textbf{\(\text{Var}(\sum X_i)\)}
but not for \textbf{exponential concentration}. Variance only gives a Chebyshev
bound (polynomial rate \(1/t^2\)), not an exponential bound.

\textbf{Conclusion}: The variance-inflated Hoeffding
is a heuristic, widely used in survey sampling and cluster randomized trials, but it is not a mathematical theorem. Writing it into the
SI will get caught by reviewers.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{2. 那 A2
本身到底站不站得住？}\label{ux90a3-a2-ux672cux8eabux5230ux5e95ux7ad9ux4e0dux7ad9ux5f97ux4f4f}

\subsubsection{2.1 更强的论证: A1 蕴含
A2}\label{ux66f4ux5f3aux7684ux8bbaux8bc1-a1-ux8574ux542b-a2}

A1 states: \(M\) experts are trained on \textbf{disjoint i.i.d. subsets}.
\[D_1, \dots, D_M \sim_{\text{i.i.d.}} P^n \quad \text{且} \quad D_i \cap D_j = \varnothing\]

Each \(f_m = \mathcal{A}(D_m)\) is a \textbf{deterministic function} of training algorithm \(\mathcal{A}\)
applied to data subset \(D_m\).

Since \(D_1, \dots, D_M\) are independent random variables, \(f_1, \dots, f_M\)
are also independent random functions.

对任意固定的测试点 \(x\):
\[e_m(x, y) = \mathbf{1}\{\ell(f_m(x), y) > \tau\}\]

因为 \(f_m\) 只依赖 \(D_m\)，且
\(D_1 \perp D_2 \perp \cdots \perp D_M\):
\[e_1 \perp e_2 \perp \cdots \perp e_M \quad \mid \quad x\]

\textbf{A2 is not an assumption --- it is a corollary of A1.}

\subsubsection{2.2
What Is the Probability Space?}\label{ux6982ux7387ux7a7aux95f4ux662fux4ec0ux4e48}

The probability space of the Hoeffding
bound is the \textbf{randomness of training data}, not the randomness of test data.

\[\mathbb{P}_{D_1,\dots,D_M}\left(C(x) > \theta \mid \text{clean}, x\right) \leq \exp(-2M(\theta-\mu_s)^2)\]

Meaning: if you randomly partition training data \(M\) times and independently train \(M\)
experts, the resulting expert ensemble has good noise-detection properties with high probability.

\textbf{After training}, you have a fixed set of experts. For a given test sample
\(x\), \(C(x)\) is a deterministic number. Hoeffding does not apply.

But this does not weaken the theorem --- all generalization bounds have this property. The theorem tells you ``this \textbf{method} is good,'' not ``this \textbf{specific ensemble} is good.''

\subsubsection{2.3 Hostile reviewer
的反驳为什么不对}\label{hostile-reviewer-ux7684ux53cdux9a73ux4e3aux4ec0ux4e48ux4e0dux5bf9}

\begin{quote}
``所有 CNN 在模糊图像上都一起错''
\end{quote}

This is saying: \textbf{given already-trained
CNNs}, their deterministic behavior on blurry images is consistent.

But what Hoeffding asks is:
\textbf{before training}, after randomly partitioning data and independently training, how confident are you of obtaining an ensemble that can detect noise?

These two are not in the same probability space. The hostile reviewer
confuses \textbf{post-training deterministic behavior} with \textbf{pre-training probabilistic guarantee}.

\subsubsection{2.4
But There Remains a Residual Issue}\label{ux4f46ux4ecdux6709ux4e00ux4e2aux6b8bux7559ux95eeux9898}

Even if A2 holds rigorously (as a corollary of A1), \textbf{estimating} \(\mu_s\)
still requires clean labels. In finite samples, errors in \(\hat{\mu}_s\) translate into
errors in \(\hat{\Delta}_s\).
This is a \textbf{finite-sample estimation problem}, not an \textbf{independence assumption problem}. Corollary
4 (finite-sample correction) already handles this with a conservative upper bound.

\subsection{3.
Honest Revision Suggestions}\label{ux8bdaux5b9eux7684ux4feeux6539ux5efaux8bae}

\subsubsection{3.1 Do Not Introduce A2'}\label{ux4e0dux8981ux5f15ux5165-a2}

The variance-inflated Hoeffding is a heuristic and is not qualified for theorem statements. Keep the original
A2, but \textbf{discuss it honestly in the main text and SI}.

\subsubsection{3.2 在 SI 中加一节 ``On the Role of Assumption
A2''}\label{ux5728-si-ux4e2dux52a0ux4e00ux8282-on-the-role-of-assumption-a2}

Contents: 1. A2 is actually a corollary of A1 (disjoint training sets → independent experts → independent errors) 2.
The probability space is training randomness, not test behavior 3. The estimation error of \(\mu_s\)
in finite samples is handled separately (Corollary 4) 4.
In practice, expert errors may exhibit superficial correlation (same blurry image), but this reflects similar \(\mu_s\) values due to feature similarity, not statistical dependence 5. Empirical diagnosis of A2:
Estimate conditional correlation coefficients of expert errors on a validation set; if they deviate significantly from zero, check whether training sets were truly disjoint

\subsubsection{3.3
Honest Wording in the Main Text}\label{ux5728ux6b63ux6587ux4e2dux8bdaux5b9eux63aaux8f9e}

Change ``conditional independence'' to: \textgreater{} ``conditional
independence of expert errors, which follows from training on disjoint
data subsets (Assumption A1)''

This downgrades A2 from ``assumption'' to ``logical consequence of A1,'' while noting that it depends on the correct execution of A1.

\subsection{5. 对 hostile review
其他批评的回应}\label{ux5bf9-hostile-review-ux5176ux4ed6ux6279ux8bc4ux7684ux56deux5e94}

\subsubsection{``CIFAR F1=0.617, 理论下界
F1≥0.976''}\label{cifar-f10.617-ux7406ux8bbaux4e0bux754c-f10.976}

This has already been honestly addressed in §4.3 (tightness discussion): \(\mu_s\) in the CIFAR experiment is approximately
0.45 (after 3-epoch CPU training), not the assumed 0.20. Plugging in the correct \(\mu_s\)
gives a lower bound of F1≥0.18, consistent with the empirical value 0.617.

\subsubsection{\texorpdfstring{``\(\mu_s\) 需要清洁标签 →
鸡生蛋''}{``\textbackslash mu\_s 需要清洁标签 → 鸡生蛋''}}\label{mu_s-ux9700ux8981ux6e05ux6d01ux6807ux7b7e-ux9e21ux751fux86cb}

The cold-start protocol requires a small number of anchor samples (Corollary 1), which is a theoretically necessary cost (Thm 3
proves unidentifiability without anchors). This is not a circular definition --- it is an engineering response to the unidentifiability theorem.
