Risk}\label{definition-1-state-conditioned-expert-risk}

\begin{quote}

\end{quote}

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{1. State space (State
Space)}\label{ux72b6ux6001ux7a7aux95f4-state-space}

\subsubsection{Definition 1.1：Status
(State)}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-1.1ux72b6ux6001-state}

A state \(s\) is a measurable subset of the input space \(\mathcal{X}\). The state partition
\(\Pi = \{s_1, s_2, \dots, s_K\}\) satisfies:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  \(\bigcup_{k=1}^K s_k = \mathcal{X}\)
\item
  \(s_k \cap s_{k'} = \emptyset\) 当 \(k \neq k'\)
\item
  每个 \(s_k\) 是 \(\mathcal{X}\) 的 Borel \(\sigma\)-代数Medium的可测集
\end{enumerate}

The mathematical essence of the state partition is finding a mapping
\(\phi: \mathcal{X} \to \mathcal{S}\) such that:

\[P(Y|X) \approx P(Y|\phi(X))\]

That is, \(\phi(X)\) is an \textbf{approximate sufficient statistic} for \(Y\) given \(X\).

\subsubsection{Definition 1.2：软Status赋值 (Soft State
Assignment)}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-1.2ux8f6fux72b6ux6001ux8d4bux503c-soft-state-assignment}

The soft state assignment function \(\gamma: \mathcal{X} \times \mathcal{S} \to [0,1]\)
satisfies:

\[\sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \gamma_s(x) = 1, \quad \forall x \in \mathcal{X}\]

Common implementations:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{GMM
  Posterior probability}：\(\gamma_s(x) = \frac{\pi_s \mathcal{N}(\phi(x) \mid \mu_s, \Sigma_s)}{\sum_{t \in \mathcal{S}} \pi_t \mathcal{N}(\phi(x) \mid \mu_t, \Sigma_t)}\)
\item
  \textbf{Kernel
  Weighted}：\(\gamma_s(x) = \frac{\kappa(\phi(x), \mu_s)}{\sum_{t \in \mathcal{S}} \kappa(\phi(x), \mu_t)}\)
\item
  \textbf{KNN 概率}：\(\gamma_s(x) = \frac{|\text{KNN}(x) \cap s|}{k}\)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{2. 条件风险 (Conditional
Risk)}\label{ux6761ux4ef6ux98ceux9669-conditional-risk}

\subsubsection{Definition 2.1：点态风险 (Pointwise
Risk)}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-2.1ux70b9ux6001ux98ceux9669-pointwise-risk}

The pointwise risk of expert \(f_m\) at input \(x\) is:

\[R_m(x) = \mathbb{E}[\ell(f_m(x), f^*(x)) \mid X = x]\]

where \(\ell: \mathcal{Y} \times \mathcal{Y} \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\)
is the loss function, and \(f^*: \mathcal{X} \to \mathcal{Y}\) is the true labeling function.

\subsubsection{Definition 2.2：State-conditioned expert risk (State-Conditioned Expert
Risk)}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-2.2ux72b6ux6001ux6761ux4ef6ux4e13ux5bb6ux98ceux9669-state-conditioned-expert-risk}

\[R_m(s) = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim P(\cdot|s)}[\ell(f_m(x), f^*(x))]\]

This is the conditional expectation in the sense of Kolmogorov. Existence is guaranteed by the Radon-Nikodym theorem:

\[R_m(s) = \frac{1}{P(X \in s)} \int_{X \in s} \ell(f_m(x), f^*(x)) \, dP_X(x)\]

When \(P(X \in s) = 0\), \(R_m(s)\) can be defined as
\(\lim_{\epsilon \to 0^+} R_m(s_\epsilon)\), where \(s_\epsilon\) is
the \(\epsilon\)-neighborhood of \(s\).

\subsubsection{Definition 2.3：Conditional Bayes risk (Conditional Bayes
Risk)}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-2.3ux6761ux4ef6ux8d1dux53f6ux65afux98ceux9669-conditional-bayes-risk}

\[R^*(s) = \inf_{f \text{ 可测}} \mathbb{E}[\ell(f(X), Y) \mid X \in s]\]

The expert risk \(R_m(s)\) measures the performance of expert \(f_m\) relative to the \textbf{unknowable optimum} \(f^*\),
rather than relative to \(R^*(s)\).

\subsubsection{Bias-Variance Decomposition}\label{ux504fux5dee-ux65b9ux5deeux5206ux89e3}

For squared loss \(\ell(\hat{y}, y) = (\hat{y} - y)^2\), let the regression function be
\(r(x) = \mathbb{E}[Y \mid X = x]\):

\[R_m(s) = \underbrace{\mathbb{E}[(f_m(X) - r(X))^2 \mid X \in s]}_{\text{Approximation error}} + \underbrace{\mathbb{E}[(r(X) - f^*(X))^2 \mid X \in s]}_{\text{Target noise}}\]

In the special case \(f^* = r\), we obtain the \textbf{conditional bias-variance decomposition}:

\[R_m(s) = \mathbb{E}[(f_m(X) - r(X))^2 \mid X \in s] + \mathbb{E}[\text{Var}(Y \mid X) \mid X \in s]\]

\subsection{3. SCX 可靠性 (SCX
Reliability)}\label{scx-ux53efux9760ux6027-scx-reliability}

\subsubsection{Definition 3.1：SCX
可靠性}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-3.1scx-ux53efux9760ux6027}

\[SCX_m(s) = P(\ell(f_m(x), f^*(x)) < \tau \mid x \in s)\]

where \(\tau > 0\) is the reliability threshold. The empirical estimate is:

\[\widehat{SCX}_m(s) = \frac{|\{x_i \in s : \ell(f_m(x_i), y_i) < \tau\}|}{|s|}\]

\subsubsection{Relationship to IRT}\label{ux4e0e-irt-ux7684ux5173ux7cfb}

The SCX \(SCX_m(s)\) can be mapped to the Rasch model:

\[\log\left(\frac{SCX_m(s)}{1 - SCX_m(s)}\right) = \theta_s - b_m\]

where \(\theta_s\) is the ''tractability'' parameter of state \(s\), and \(b_m\) is the ability threshold of expert \(m\).

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{5. noise与Learnability (Noise \&
Learnability)}\label{ux566aux58f0ux4e0eux53efux5b66ux4e60ux6027-noise-learnability}

\subsubsection{Definition
5.1：noise分数}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-5.1ux566aux58f0ux5206ux6570}

\[\text{NoiseScore}(x_i) = r_i \cdot \frac{1}{\rho(s_i) + \varepsilon} \cdot [1 - C(s_i)]\]

where \(C(s)\) is the internal consistency of state \(s\):

\[C(s) = 1 - \frac{1}{|s|} \sum_{x_i \in s} \frac{|\ell(f_m(x_i), y_i) - \bar{\ell}(s)|}{\max \ell - \min \ell}\]

\subsubsection{Definition
5.2：Learnability}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-5.2ux53efux5b66ux4e60ux6027}

\[L(s) = \inf_{f \in \mathcal{F}} \left[ \frac{R(f|s) - R^*(s)}{R^*(s)} \right]^{-1}\]

where
\(R(f|s) = \mathbb{E}[\ell(f(X), Y) | X \in s]\), and \(R^*(s) = \inf_f R(f|s)\)
is the Bayes risk. \(L(s) \to 0\) indicates that the state is unlearnable under the current function class \(\mathcal{F}\).

\subsubsection{Definition
5.3：Redundancy}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-5.3ux5197ux4f59ux5ea6}

State redundancy estimate:

\[D(s) = \rho(s) \cdot \bigl(1 - \bar{r}(s)\bigr) \cdot \text{Sim}(s) \cdot \bigl(1 - \text{Boundary}(s)\bigr)\]

where \(\text{Sim}(s)\) is the intra-state similarity, and \(\text{Boundary}(s)\)
is the proportion of boundary samples.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{6. Expert Routing (Expert
Routing)}\label{ux4e13ux5bb6ux8defux7531-expert-routing}

\subsubsection{Definition
6.1：Status条件专家权重}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-6.1ux72b6ux6001ux6761ux4ef6ux4e13ux5bb6ux6743ux91cd}

\[w_m(x) \propto \exp\left(-\alpha \cdot \sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \gamma_s(x) \cdot \hat{R}_m(s)\right)\]

Normalization ensures \(\sum_{m=1}^M w_m(x) = 1\), with \(\alpha > 0\) as the inverse temperature parameter.

\subsubsection{Definition
6.2：Hard Routing}\label{ux5b9aux4e49-6.2ux786cux8defux7531}

\[m^*(x) = \arg\min_{m \in [M]} \sum_{s \in \mathcal{S}} \gamma_s(x) \cdot R_m(s)\]

\subsection{7. Convergence Properties}\label{ux6536ux655bux6027ux8d28}

\subsubsection{Theorem
7.1：Concentration of Empirical Risk Estimates}\label{ux5b9aux7406-7.1ux7ecfux9a8cux98ceux9669ux4f30ux8ba1ux7684ux96c6ux4e2dux6027}

Let \(\ell \in [0, L_{\max}]\) and let state \(s\) have \(n_s\) samples. For any
\(\delta > 0\), with probability at least \(1 - \delta\):

\[|\hat{R}_m(s) - R_m(s)| \leq L_{\max} \sqrt{\frac{\log(2/\delta)}{2n_s}}\]

\subsubsection{Theorem
7.2：Minimax Lower Bound}\label{ux5b9aux7406-7.2ux6700ux5c0fux6700ux5927ux4e0bux754c}

There exists a distribution such that any estimator \(\hat{R}_m(s)\) satisfies:

\[\mathbb{E}[|\hat{R}_m(s) - R_m(s)|] \geq \frac{\sigma(s)}{2\sqrt{n_s}}\]

where \(\sigma^2(s) = \text{Var}(\ell(f_m(X), f^*(X)) \mid X \in s)\).
