\subsubsection{5.1 Limitations to Acknowledge
(Required)}\label{limitations-to-acknowledge-required}

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\item
  \textbf{Reference-set replay requirement (Theorem 12.5).} The Lyapunov
  descent proof requires either importance sampling (for the student) or
  reference-set-based SCXUpdate computation (for the gatekeeper).
  Without these, Lyapunov descent is \textbf{provably impossible} in the
  asymptotic regime (Theorem 12.2). This should be stated clearly in the
  abstract and introduction.
\item
  \textbf{Finite covering dimension vs.~finite \(\mathcal{X}\).} The
  memory bank stabilization proof (Lemma SE-1.2) uses a covering-number
  argument that guarantees \(\varepsilon\)-precision convergence. Full
  functional convergence (in \(\ell^\infty\) norm) for infinite
  \(\mathcal{X}\) remains an open problem requiring additional
  compactness conditions.
\item
  \textbf{\(K>2\) construction in Theorem 3.} The \(K\)-class
  generalization of Theorem 3 uses ``completely random experts'' --- an
  extreme form of difficulty. The practically relevant construction is
  the \(K=2\) case. The \(K>2\) case is included for mathematical
  completeness as an existence proof.
\item
  \textbf{Regime 3 (Perpetual Discovery) and Phase Diagram.} These are
  explicitly marked as \textbf{conjectured}. They are not needed for the
  main convergence and completeness theorems but are included to
  characterize the full behavioral landscape of the self-evolution
  system.
\item
  \textbf{Importance weight variance.} Theorem 12.5's importance
  sampling mechanism inflates gradient variance by factor
  \(W \leq 1/\varepsilon\). For small exploration fractions
  \(\varepsilon\), this may cause slow convergence in practice. The
  theoretical guarantee (descent in expectation) holds, but
  finite-sample convergence rates may be degraded.
\item
  \textbf{Cold-start requirement.} Theorem 3 proves that initial human
  review (anchor points) is a \textbf{theoretical necessity}, not an
  engineering compromise. Any method claiming to identify label noise
  without any ground-truth labels is implicitly assuming at least as
  much structure as A1-A6.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{5.2 Strengths to Emphasize}\label{strengths-to-emphasize}

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\item
  \textbf{Closed theoretical loop.} Theorems 1-3 form a complete
  necessity-sufficiency-boundary triad:

  \begin{itemize}
  \tightlist
  \item
    Thm 3: Without A1-A6, noise detection is ill-posed
  \item
    Thm 1: With A1-A6, noise detection succeeds exponentially
  \item
    Thm 2: Even with A1-A6, weak features bound SCX's advantage
  \end{itemize}
\item
  \textbf{Provable self-evolution convergence.} Spring-1 and Spring-2
  provide the first (to our knowledge) formal convergence guarantees for
  a self-improving ML system with a learned data filter, including a
  finite-time termination bound.
\item
  \textbf{All assumptions are testable.} Every assumption (A1-A6, C1-C9)
  has an associated statistical diagnostic test with explicit thresholds
  (see Thm 3 Section 6.3, Thm 2 Section 7).
\item
  \textbf{Bretagnolle-Huber tightens Pinsker.} Theorem 2 uses the BH
  inequality to sharpen the standard Pinsker bound, providing tighter
  constants by factor \(\sim 1.4\times\) in the practically relevant
  \(\delta\) regime.
\item
  \textbf{Reference-set replay mechanism.} The mechanism that closes the
  Lyapunov gap (Theorem 12.5) is both theoretically sound and
  practically implementable --- it requires only a small holdout set and
  standard importance sampling techniques.
\end{enumerate}
